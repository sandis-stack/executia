§ 03 — The Solution

A protocol for verified execution.

◎

Responsibility Assignment

Every decision must have a clearly assigned responsible actor before execution can begin. No assignment — no execution.

◈

Verification Layer

Execution cannot proceed without confirmation from the responsible party. Enforced at the structural level — not as policy, but as impossibility.

◉

Execution Integrity

Every step is traceable and auditable. The complete chain — decision, verification, execution, result — is written permanently.

§ 04 — Protocol Specification

How it works.

SPEC-01 · Input

Decision Registered

An institutional decision is captured and formalised as a verifiable record with authority, scope, and conditions attached.

↓
SPEC-02 · Core ◆

◎ Responsible Actor Assigned

Authority confirmed. Budget integrity checked. Responsibility is cryptographically linked to the decision. No bypass. No exception path.

↓
SPEC-03 · Gate

Verification Required

The responsible actor must confirm execution. Without confirmation, no signal is emitted to downstream systems. Execution is structurally blocked.

↓
SPEC-04 · Ledger

Execution Confirmed

Only after full verification does EXECUTIA emit a signal. The complete chain is written to an immutable ledger. Cannot be altered.
